# Information Ratio Bounds

Use these local moves when a proof routes regret through target information, delayed information gain, posterior-sampling concentration, or IDS ratio objectives.

### Shortfall telescoping against a baseline policy

- Category: `local-move`
- Use when: A regret proof needs to replace a global performance gap by one-step shortfall terms along the learner's trajectory.
- Required assumptions: Finite horizon, value functions `V_pi` and `Q_pi` for the baseline policy, and Bellman consistency for `Q_pi`.
- Core move: Write the value gap as a telescoping sum of `V_pi(H_t) - Q_pi(H_t,A_t)` terms.
- Self-contained statement or template: If actions are generated by the learner but the benchmark value is `V_pi`, then `V_pi - V_agent` equals the learner-trajectory expectation of the sum of baseline shortfalls, up to the sign convention used for rewards.
- How to adapt: Define the baseline first, expand `V_pi(H_0) - sum_t R_{t+1}`, add and subtract next-step values, and condition on `(H_t,A_t)` to identify `Q_pi`.
- Failure modes: The sign flips if the proof defines regret as `V_agent - V_pi`. Infinite-horizon or discounted settings need a terminal or discount-tail argument.
- Related entries: Information-ratio regret from retained target information; Tau-step retained-information telescope.
- Material sources: `RLB23-D01`.
- Public attribution: Lu, Van Roy, Dwaracherla, Ibrahimi, Osband, and Wen 2023.

### Tau-step retained-information telescope

- Category: `local-move`
- Use when: An information-ratio proof measures information gained over a `tau`-step window rather than the next single observation.
- Required assumptions: `I_t = I(chi;E|P_t)` is nonnegative and nonincreasing in `t`.
- Core move: Bound `(1/tau) sum_t (I_t - I_{t+tau})` by the initial target information `I_0`.
- Self-contained statement or template: Expand each delayed difference into `tau` one-step differences, swap the sums, telescope each shifted chain, drop nonnegative tails, and use monotonicity to get at most `I_0`.
- How to adapt: State what epistemic state `P_t` retains, prove monotonicity or replace it with a supermartingale-style retained-information bound, then apply the same index shift.
- Failure modes: If the epistemic state forgets target-relevant information, monotonicity can fail. Then the bound needs a retained-information accounting term.
- Related entries: Shortfall telescoping against a baseline policy; Information-ratio regret from retained target information.
- Material sources: `RLB23-D02`, `RLB23-S01`.
- Public attribution: Lu, Van Roy, Dwaracherla, Ibrahimi, Osband, and Wen 2023.

### Quantized posterior concentration from information gain

- Category: `local-move`
- Use when: A posterior-sampling proof has continuous parameters but the regret framework needs a finite-entropy learning target.
- Required assumptions: Posterior components with a concentration inequality comparable to the Beta-Bernoulli setting, independent posterior samples or a controllable coupling, and a quantization grid with residual `delta`.
- Core move: Quantize the posterior target, use information gain to control sampled-parameter deviation, and pay explicit quantization residuals.
- Self-contained statement or template: A source-specific concentration bound gives `|p - p_hat|` controlled by a square-root information term. Quantizing `p` to `q` reduces target entropy and adds at most a grid residual; a union bound supplies the multi-component log factor.
- How to adapt: Reprove the posterior-family concentration constant before reusing the template. Then choose `delta` by balancing entropy/log factors against the residual bias in the regret bound.
- Failure modes: The Beta constants do not transfer to arbitrary posterior families. Quantization can silently dominate the final rate if residuals are not balanced.
- Related entries: Quantized target for posterior-sampling regret.
- Material sources: `RLB23-D03`.
- Public attribution: Lu, Van Roy, Dwaracherla, Ibrahimi, Osband, and Wen 2023.

### Two-action support for IDS ratio objectives

- Category: `local-move`
- Use when: An information-directed sampling objective optimizes a ratio of squared expected shortfall to expected information gain over action mixtures.
- Required assumptions: Objective of the form `(nu^T alpha)^2 / (nu^T beta)` on a simplex, with positive denominator on feasible mixtures.
- Core move: Use quadratic-over-linear convexity and support reduction to restrict an optimal mixture to at most two actions.
- Self-contained statement or template: The map `(x,y) -> x^2/y` is convex for `y > 0`; composing with `(nu^T alpha, nu^T beta)` gives convexity in `nu`. Since the objective depends on the mixture only through two linear statistics, an optimal simplex point exists on a two-action face.
- How to adapt: Identify `alpha` as action shortfall and `beta` as action information gain. Remove actions with zero feasible information gain or handle them by limiting arguments.
- Failure modes: If the denominator can be zero at the optimum, the ratio needs a convention or regularization. Extra nonlinear constraints can break the two-action support argument.
- Related entries: Conditional IDS ratio induction; Variance lower bound for pseudo-information gain.
- Material sources: `RLB23-D04`.
- Public attribution: Lu, Van Roy, Dwaracherla, Ibrahimi, Osband, and Wen 2023.

### Variance lower bound for pseudo-information gain

- Category: `local-move`
- Use when: A value-IDS proof wants to replace mutual information about pseudo-observations by a variance or covariance proxy.
- Required assumptions: Pseudo-observation components have bounded span, information is measured in nats, and the target affects conditional means of the pseudo-observation.
- Core move: Apply chain rule, componentwise KL, Pinsker's inequality, and a bounded-span mean-separation bound to get information at least proportional to a covariance trace.
- Self-contained statement or template: For bounded pseudo-observations `Y_i`, `I(target;Y)` lower-bounds `sum_i Var(E[Y_i|target])` up to a span-squared constant; vectorizing gives a trace covariance term.
- How to adapt: Verify the component span bound for both `Q_dagger` and the added pseudo-observation noise. Track log base, since constants differ between nats and bits.
- Failure modes: Heavy-tailed or unbounded pseudo-observations need a different inequality. The variance proxy can be misleading if the target changes distributions without changing conditional means.
- Related entries: Two-action support for IDS ratio objectives; Conditional IDS ratio induction.
- Material sources: `RLB23-D05`.
- Public attribution: Lu, Van Roy, Dwaracherla, Ibrahimi, Osband, and Wen 2023.
